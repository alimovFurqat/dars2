Dr. Norman Borlaug

The man who saved a billion lives




Dr. Norman Borlaug, third from the left, trains biologists in Mehico on how to increase wheat yields-part of his life-long war on hunger

Here's a time line of Dr. Borlaug's life:

  1. 1914 - Born in Creco, lowa

  2. 1933 - Leaves his family's farm to attend the University of Minnesota, thanks to a Depression era program known as the "National Youth Administation"

  3. 1935 - Has to stop school and save up more money. Works in the civilian conservation corps, helping starving Americans. "I saw how food changed them", he said. "All of this left scars on me."

  4. 1937- Finishes university and takes a job in the US Forestry Service

  5. 1942- Receives a Ph.D.in Genetics and Plant Pathology

  6. 1945- Discovers a way to grown wheat twice each season, doubling wheat yields

  7. 1962- Visit Delhi and brings his high-yielding strains of wheat to the Indian subcontinent in time to help mitigate mass starvation due to a rapidly expanding population

  8. 1970- receives the Nobel Peace Prize

  9. 1983- helps seven African countries dramatically increase their maize and sorghum yields

  10. 1984- becomes a distinguished professor at Texas A&M University

  11. 2009- dies at the age of 95.
